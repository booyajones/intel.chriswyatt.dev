2. The Diagnosis: Contextual Schizophrenia

When we audited his feed, the problem wasn't that his posts lacked "hooks." The problem was structural and strategic.

  • He was positioned as a practitioner, not a visionary. Every post was about "teamwork" and "attending events." His Board of Directors needed him positioned as a category kingmaker. Every PR post widened that gap.
  • He was writing for his peers, not his buyers. He was trying to relate to other startup founders. But his software is sold to enterprise CFOs. A CFO does not care about your startup's resilience; they care about their own balance sheet.
  • The tone was deeply un-executive. The emojis, the exclamation points, the "thrilled to announce" framing—it signaled junior marketing, not executive authority.
